# 📊 Crypto Sentiment vs Trader Performance Analysis

## 🔍 Overview
This project explores the relationship between market sentiment (Fear & Greed Index) and trader performance using historical trading data from Hyperliquid.

The goal is to understand how trader behavior and profitability change under different market conditions and uncover patterns that can inform smarter trading strategies.

---

## 📂 Dataset
The analysis uses two datasets:
- **Fear & Greed Index Dataset** → Market sentiment (Fear, Greed, etc.)
- **Historical Trader Data** → Trade details including price, size, leverage, and profit/loss

---

## 📊 Key Analysis Performed
- Profit & Loss (PnL) analysis across sentiment categories  
- Win rate comparison under different market conditions  
- Trade count distribution vs sentiment  
- Coin-wise trader participation analysis  
- Heatmaps for trader vs coin profitability  
- Time-series analysis of trading activity and sentiment trends  

---

## 📈 Key Insights
- Traders perform best during **Greed / Extreme Greed** conditions  
- **Fear markets** show higher trading activity but lower win rates  
- Trading activity is concentrated in a small number of coins  
- Profit distribution is highly skewed with a few large trades driving overall gains

## 🎯 Conclusion
Market sentiment has a significant impact on trader performance. Combining sentiment analysis with trading strategies can improve decision-making by adapting to changing market conditions.
